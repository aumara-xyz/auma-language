# The Vision — Uniting Humanity Through Language

> *auma esi dona-iva a tuli — Auma is given to you all.*

This document is the long-form answer to one question: **why build a language?**

The living version lives at [auma.one/vision](https://auma.one/vision). Listen first if you prefer: [A Language Where Grammar Prevents Lying](https://www.aumara.xyz/auma) — a 40-minute immersive introduction.

## 1 · Language is the operating system of thought

Every system of thought runs on a language. Before you can think a thing clearly, your language has to make that thought easy to hold.

Linguists call it the **Sapir-Whorf hypothesis** — linguistic relativity: the language you speak shapes what you can easily think. The strong form (language strictly determines thought) is debated. The weak form (language nudges attention, memory, and habit) is well supported by decades of research on color terms, spatial reasoning, and grammatical gender.

AUMA takes the hypothesis as an **invitation**, not a doctrine:

> If language shapes thought, then build a language where clarity is the path of least resistance — where honesty is easier than deception, where connection is grammatical.

In a fractured world, a shared language is medicine. Not because it erases difference — because it gives difference a common place to meet.

## 2 · The 150-year dream

In 1887, L. L. Zamenhof — a Jewish oculist from Białystok, a town split between four languages and their resentments — published **Esperanto**: a language belonging to no nation, designed so that no speaker would ever hold home-field advantage over another. Millions learned it. It survived two world wars and the empires that banned it.

The dream did not fail. It was early.

Zamenhof built for a world of letters and border posts. AUMA is built for the world that actually arrived — one where **humans and artificial minds share every conversation**, and where the new common language must be learnable by both.

## 3 · A grammar where honesty is syntax

AUMA's deepest design choice is **evidentiality**: the grammar asks a speaker to mark *how they know* what they claim. Not as politeness — as syntax. Four small words, met on day 54 of the Journey:

| word | spoken | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `nuna` | noo-nah | I know by seeing |
| `odi` | oh-dee | I know by hearing |
| `padi` | pah-dee | I know by reasoning |
| `intu` | een-too | I know by intuition |

For humans, this is a practice of self-honesty — you feel the difference between what you saw and what you assumed.

For artificial minds, it is something stronger: **a language in which every claim declares its source.** An AI that speaks AUMA fluently cannot blur "I verified this" into "this sounds right." *Hallucination becomes a syntax error.*

Natural languages weren't designed for AI honesty. AUMA is.

## 4 · Radically learnable, on purpose

- **84 days** from first sentence to graduation
- **~950 words** in the living canon
- **1 sentence shape** — who · does · what — never broken
- **0 exceptions** — every letter always sounds the same; nothing conjugates

Research on Esperanto learners found something beautiful — the *propaedeutic effect*: a transparent constructed language doesn't just teach itself quickly, it **accelerates every language learned after it**. A first language that shows you how languages work is a gift that keeps paying forward.

## 5 · An open-source language, owned by everyone

A language held in secret is a contradiction. So the entire canon — every word, the frozen grammar, the 84-day curriculum, the graded readers, the governance — is:

- **CC BY-SA licensed** (the Wikipedia license)
- **Versioned like software** (v13.1 → v14 → v15 → v16 → …)
- **Machine-linted** — `tools/aumaCanonLint.ts` must pass with 0 errors before any release ships
- **Governed in three rings** — seed core (nearly immutable), living canon (versioned releases), community garden (your proposals)

The dream is not a language published *at* the world. It is one the world grows with us:

1. **Learn it** at [auma.one](https://auma.one) — free, no account, forever
2. **Speak it** — teach one word to one person; that's the whole engine
3. **Grow it** — [propose a word](https://github.com/aumara-xyz/auma-language/issues/new/choose); the garden is where the language lives
4. **Build with it** — the canon is machine-readable; make things we haven't imagined

## 6 · How it spreads

No marketing budget spreads a language. People do. Someone learns `salama`, says it to a friend, and the friend asks what it means. **It starts with two people.**

Every day, one small transmission of the language goes out into the world — a word, a pattern, a sentence — free to be shared, remixed, and taught onward.

This is a gift, not a product. No account. No paywall. No lock-in.
