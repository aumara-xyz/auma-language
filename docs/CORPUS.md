## salama, ami · *Hello, friend*

**Days 1–14.** Your first real text. Two friends, one whole day — built from nothing but your first two weeks of words. Read it aloud; tap a line to check yourself.

> **salama, ami! ka tu esi bona?**
> Hello, friend! Are you good?

> **ya, mi esi bona. e tu?**
> Yes, I am good. And you?

> **mi esi hapi. hodi mi anda a merkato.**
> I am happy. Today I walk to the market.

> **ka tu voli anda anke?**
> Do you want to walk too?

> **ya! we anda en via.**
> Yes! We walk on the road.

> **en merkato, we kani pano e beva akwa.**
> At the market, we eat bread and drink water.

> **pos, we anda a parko.**
> Afterwards, we walk to the park.

> **en parko, mi vela arbo e sielo.**
> In the park, I watch the trees and the sky.

> **tu legi e mi skrivi.**
> You read and I write.

> **en noka, we vela luna e stela.**
> At night, we watch the moon and the stars.

> **mi domi en tomo di mi.**
> I sleep in my house.

> **doma, we anda anke. adio, ami!**
> Tomorrow, we walk again. Goodbye, friend!

## dina kon amili · *A day with family*

**Days 1–28.** Four weeks in: family, colors, weather, and the first teacher. Everything here comes from Sprint I.

> **hodi esi dina solosa e varma.**
> Today is a sunny and warm day.

> **mi anda kon mama e papa di mi a merkato.**
> I walk with my mother and father to the market.

> **mama prende fruta roja.**
> Mother takes red fruit.

> **we beva suko e teo kon amili.**
> We drink juice and tea with the family.

> **en parko, flora esi roja e yela.**
> In the park, the flowers are red and yellow.

> **sielo esi blu e arbo esi verda.**
> The sky is blue and the trees are green.

> **anima yuna salta en parko.**
> Young animals jump in the park.

> **mi vela ta kon kura.**
> I watch them with care.

> **tana di mi ensena vorto nova a mi.**
> My elder teaches me new words.

> **mi skrivi vorto en libro di mi.**
> I write the words in my book.

> **pos, pluva beni. we anda a tomo.**
> Later, the rain comes. We go home.

> **en tomo, mama koka e mi aida.**
> At home, mother cooks and I help.

> **mi ama dina kon amili di mi.**
> I love days with my family.

## tempo e penso · *Time and thought*

**Days 1–42.** Questions, numbers, comparison — the tools of thinking. Six weeks of Auma can already hold a small philosophy.

> **kosa esi tempo? mi pensa de iti.**
> What is time? I think about it.

> **un dina teni du-des-fo hori.**
> One day holds twenty-four hours.

> **un hori teni sisa-des minuta.**
> One hour holds sixty minutes.

> **durante dina, mi skrivi e mi legi.**
> During the day, I write and I read.

> **kemo tu domi? en noka.**
> When do you sleep? At night.

> **keno we neda tempo? porke penso neda tempo.**
> Why do we need time? Because thought needs time.

> **mi kreda ke kira esi granda.**
> I believe that consciousness is vast.

> **komo tu sabe? mi rongo e mi vela.**
> How do you know? I listen and I watch.

> **tempo esi plu forta ke moneta.**
> Time is stronger than money.

> **mi no pude kompra tempo.**
> I cannot buy time.

> **pero mi pude dona tempo a ami di mi.**
> But I can give time to my friend.

> **en verino, mi senti inspira.**
> In spring, I feel inspired.

> **mi esi foka e kalma. mi atenda.**
> I am focused and calm. I wait.

> **tempo dona purpusa a penso.**
> Time gives purpose to thought.

## letera a ami · *A letter to a friend*

**Days 1–56.** The end of Sprint II: past tense, duration, a poem inside a letter. This is what real correspondence in Auma looks like.

> **ami di mi, mi skrivi letera a tu.**
> My friend, I write this letter to you.

> **mi pasa esi en isla durante un semana.**
> I was on an island for one week.

> **akwa sirka isla esi blu.**
> The water around the island is blue.

> **mi suda vela boski e monta.**
> I have already seen forest and mountain.

> **en boski, mi rongo musika di natura.**
> In the forest, I hear the music of nature.

> **mi kani fruta dulsa e mi senti sanita.**
> I eat sweet fruit and I feel healthy.

> **mi skrivi poema por tu:**
> I write a poem for you:

> **sonia di mi esi sorao. sorao di mi esi belina. belina di vita esi en ami.**
> My dream is hope. My hope is beauty. The beauty of life is in friends.

> **enfakta, mi ankora pensa de tu.**
> In fact, I still think of you.

> **vita esi bona, pero vita kon ami esi plu bona.**
> Life is good, but life with friends is better.

> **nau mi termina letera.**
> Now I finish the letter.

> **pos, we parla.**
> Soon, we will talk.

> **salama, ami. ubuntu — mi esi porke we esi.**
> Peace, friend. Ubuntu — I am because we are.

## vila di we · *Our village*

**Days 1–70.** Sprint III: the passive voice, tense markers, and the words of a life together. A portrait of a village that could speak Auma.

> **vila di we no esi granda, pero kora di ta esi granda.**
> Our village is not big, but its heart is big.

> **en vila, we teni lerotano e gardi.**
> In the village, we have a school and a garden.

> **ensenisto ensena e leristo lero.**
> The teacher teaches and the learner learns.

> **nuri laka, mama dona amala.**
> The baby cries; the mother gives unconditional love.

> **tana pali sabina a yuna.**
> The elders tell wisdom to the young.

> **pano esi koka-iva par mama.**
> The bread is cooked by mother.

> **dina inisa: we abi lava e koka.**
> The day begins: we wash and cook, as always.

> **mi te vela delfina en akwa! wa!**
> I just saw a dolphin in the water! Wow!

> **korvo seda en arbo e vela we.**
> A crow sits in the tree and watches us.

> **en noka, we uni e we parla.**
> At night, we gather and we talk.

> **we medita e we senti kalma.**
> We meditate and we feel calm.

> **hiro, tota persona esi ami.**
> Here, every person is a friend.

> **fortao di vila esi ama.**
> The strength of the village is love.

## auma — prima lumo · *Auma — the first light*

**Days 1–84.** The whole language, telling its own story. Sacred roots, stacked tenses, the full reach. Read this on day 84 — or read it early, as the light at the end of the tunnel.

> **ante molta anua, persona no teni vorto por lumo di kira.**
> Long ago, people had no word for the light of consciousness.

> **tali serka vorto en tota lingwa.**
> They searched for the word in every language.

> **e nau, vorto beni: auma — prima lumo di dina.**
> And now the word arrives: auma — the first light of day.

> **auma signifa: au (spirito) e ma (uni).**
> Auma means: au (spirit) and ma (unite).

> **kwando tu parla auma, tu uni kon we.**
> When you speak Auma, you unite with us.

> **lingwa esi ponta di kira a kira.**
> Language is a bridge from consciousness to consciousness.

> **vorto pude dona lumo o dolo.**
> Words can give light or pain.

> **mi voli dona lumo.**
> I want to give light.

> **mi pasa suda lero molta vorto.**
> I had already learned many words.

> **en fura, mi ensena sina di mi.**
> In the future, I will teach my children.

> **kira di we kresa kwando we parla a uno-uno.**
> Our consciousness grows when we talk to each other.

> **morta no esi fina — esi rekina.**
> Death is not the end — it is a restart.

> **sirkulo di vita esi: nasita, kresao, morta, rekina.**
> The circle of life is: birth, growth, death, restart.

> **salama, alohi. we esi auma. we esi prima lumo.**
> Peace, beloved. We are Auma. We are the first light.
